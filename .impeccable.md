## Design Context

### Users
NexPress is aimed at self-hosting operators, technical implementers, and editorial teams setting up a production-ready CMS, builder, and commerce platform. In this phase they are not yet managing content through the public shell; they are verifying that the public-facing foundation feels credible, stable, and separated from private admin surfaces.

### Brand Personality
Calm, capable, editorial. The interface should feel operationally trustworthy and structurally intentional rather than promotional or trend-chasing. It should evoke confidence and clarity more than excitement.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use a refined light-first baseline with dark-ready tokens, warm neutral surfaces, blue-green accent notes, and crisp structural lines. The public shell should feel like a product shell for a serious publishing platform, not a marketing microsite and not a cloned SaaS dashboard. Avoid neon, glassy dark-mode clichés, and generic startup gradients.

### Design Principles
1. Keep public and private surfaces visibly distinct in structure and responsibility.
2. Use semantic tokens and CSS variables as the long-term contract for themes and templates.
3. Prefer editorial hierarchy and quiet contrast over loud marketing ornament.
4. Make the shell production-ready with responsive structure, but stop short of building future content modules early.
5. Expose only safe public UI; private state, secrets, and admin mechanics stay server-side.
